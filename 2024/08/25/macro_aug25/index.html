Markets Recap - Anticipations of Central Bank Policy Shifts

Summary

  • US markets gained this week, driven by growing expectations of an imminent interest rate cut and positive sentiment following Fed Chair Powell’s speech. Similarly, The EU stock markets rose this week, driven by expectations of an ECB interest rate cut and improved business activity, while bond yields fell in response to growing calls for monetary easing.

  • Asian markets saw modest gains in Japan due to expectations of rate hikes by the BoJ, while Chinese stocks underperformed amid cautious sentiment and weak earnings reports.

  • This week, the Japanese yen strengthened and the dollar weakened amid shifting central bank expectations, while oil prices rose due to anticipated Fed rate cuts but fell overall due to demand concerns, and gold saw modest gains from increased demand for non-interest-bearing assets.


US Market :

This week, US stock markets saw gains as expectations grew for potential interest rate cuts, with both the Dow Jones Industrial Average and the S&P 500 nearing record levels. A key driver of market sentiment was Fed Chair Jerome Powell’s speech at the Kansas City Fed’s annual economic symposium in Jackson Hole. His acknowledgment that “the time has come for policy to adjust” signaled the likelihood of a rate cut in September, boosting investor confidence and lifting stocks at the start of trading.


In the bond market, this optimism led to a drop in the 10-year Treasury yield, while longer-term yields fell, and Treasuries rallied after the Labor Department revised annual job growth estimates downward by 818,000 jobs—the largest downward adjustment since 2009.


Economic Data

  • S&P Global’s first estimate for August showed the manufacturing sector remained weak, while the services sector continued its solid expansion

  • Existing home sales rose slightly, as expected, breaking a four-month streak of declines

  • The Labor Department’s annual revision of nonfarm payroll data revealed 818,000 fewer jobs added over the past 12 months than initially reported

EU Market :

European stock markets followed the US lead, buoyed by expectations of an ECB interest rate cut. The pan-European STOXX Europe 600 Index rose by 1.31%, with major indexes also posting gains: Germany’s DAX increased by 1.70%, France’s CAC 40 climbed 1.71%, and Italy’s FTSE MIB advanced 1.84%. The UK’s FTSE 100 saw a more modest rise of 0.20%. Eurozone business activity also showed improvement, with S&P Global’s PMI indicating a rebound in August after stalling in July, driven in part by the Olympic Games boosting France’s services sector.


In the bond market, Eurozone government bond yields fell in tandem with the US, as calls for a potential ECB rate cut grew louder. Bank of Finland Governor Olli Rehn and Italy’s Governor Fabio Panetta both noted that the case for a September rate cut had strengthened, while Latvia’s central bank governor Martins Kazaks suggested the possibility of two further rate cuts this year.


Economic Data

  • UK’s flash PMI in August shows fastest rise in private sector output

  • Eurozone flash PMI for August indicates rise in business activity

  • Germany’s flash PMI for August shows private sector in contraction

  • France flash PMI numbers for August indicate expansion in services

  • Euro Area inflation rate rises to 2.6% in July

  • Spain’s trade deficit shrinks to €0.7B

Asian Market :

Japanese stock markets experienced modest gains this week, supported by expectations of potential rate hikes from the Bank of Japan. The Nikkei 225 Index rose by 0.8%. Despite recent market turbulence raising speculation that the BoJ might pause its rate hikes, Governor Kazuo Ueda confirmed the central bank’s commitment to normalizing monetary policy as it seeks to achieve stable 2% inflation. Core consumer price inflation accelerated for the third consecutive month in July, reinforcing the BoJ’s hawkish stance.


In China, the stock market underperformed amid a quiet economic calendar and cautious sentiment ahead of Fed Chair Powell’s Jackson Hole speech. Both the Shanghai Composite and CSI 300 Indexes posted weekly declines. In corporate news, Baidu reported second-quarter revenue below expectations, although its earnings surpassed analysts’ forecasts.


Economic Data :

  • Japan’s core inflation rate accelerates for third consecutive month to 2.7% in July

  • Japan’s manufacturing PMI contracts more than expected; robust services grow at 4-month high

  • Japan’s trade deficit widens in July; exports grow below estimates, imports accelerate

  • China keeps loan prime rate unchanged after surprise cut in July

  • China stops releasing real-time foreign fund flow data

Other Markets :

This week, the EU currency appreciated and the dollar depreciated due to shifting expectations for central bank policies. Notably, the Japanese yen strengthened, moving to the high end of the JPY 144 range against the USD, up from approximately JPY 147 at the previous week’s close.


WTI crude oil futures rose to $74.83 per barrel on Friday, driven by Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell’s comments suggesting potential interest rate cuts, which typically boost oil demand. Despite this, oil prices fell over 2% for the week due to ongoing concerns about slowing energy demand, as indicated by weaker-than-expected US manufacturing activity reported in the latest S&P PMI data.


Gold prices saw modest gains as expectations of a dovish Fed increased demand for non-interest-bearing assets. Chairman Jerome Powell’s signals during the Jackson Hole Economic Symposium suggested the central bank might lower interest rates in response to a softer labor market and slowing inflation, supporting gold’s appeal.
